Artisan Software

I fell in love with computers before knowing how to read. In middle school, my nights were spent watching computer science and interface design lectures from Stanford and MIT. By high school graduation, my app development work was featured on TV, in newspapers, and on over a million devices. Coding my way through college, I expanded my expertise into web development and data science. Today, I draw upon these experiences in the creation of what I call “Artisan Software.”

Creating software isn't a purely technical discipline - it's an art. Artisan software requires an intentional balance of design, user psychology, sustainable engineering, and business sense. It's what separates your favorite cafe from Panera Bread. I've spent the last X years creating artisan software and am here for anyone who would like to join me.
